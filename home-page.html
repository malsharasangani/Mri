Magnetic Resonance Tomography (MRI)

Magnetic resonance tomography (formerly nuclear magnetic resonance tomography) produces cross-sectional images of the human body by changing magnetic fields. It does not require x-rays and provides more contrasting images of soft tissue than computed tomography, but also takes longer (examination times 15-45 minutes). Since very strong magnetic fields are used, the physician must be informed of possible metallic foreign bodies.
